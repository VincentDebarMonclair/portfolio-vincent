How I got into dev

My journey into web development began with a simple question: “What if I could create things people actually use every day?” After years in the sports industry, I was ready for a new kind of challenge-one that would let me build and understand the digital world from the ground up.

At first, learning to code felt a bit like training for a marathon: demanding, sometimes confusing, but incredibly rewarding. I started with the basics of HTML and CSS, then quickly dove into JavaScript, eager to make things interactive. Before I knew it, I was exploring both the front-end and the back-end-discovering how much fun it is to design sleek user interfaces and also to build the logic and data behind the scenes.

What I love about being a fullstack developer is the variety: one day I’m crafting pixel-perfect layouts, the next I’m designing APIs or optimizing databases. It’s the perfect playground for my curiosity and creativity. Switching careers wasn’t always easy, but my drive to learn and adapt-skills I honed in the sports world-helped me push through. Today, I’m excited to bring that same energy and teamwork to every project, whether it’s building the interface, the engine, or both.
